Decoding a Cure.

Hosted by
M. Hale & P. Anand
Slow conversations about the messy, hopeful business of teaching machines to read a body.
Listen anywhere podcasts live· or right here

W e started this show because the room where AI meets medicine is full of jargon and short on warmth. So we pulled up two armchairs, lit a lamp, and asked the people building the future to explain it the way they would to a friend over dinner.

No tickers. No press releases. Just professors, residents, and the occasional patient — talking about the small, strange tools that are already changing how care happens.

Departments
4 of 4
Department I

Catching the bleed

Software that reads brain scans alongside the on-call radiologist. We talk about the night shift, the false alarms, and the few cases where minutes change a life.

Department II

A phone for an eye

Glaucoma quietly takes vision before the patient notices. We meet the people building screening tools small enough to fit in a coat pocket and reach a rural clinic.

Department III

Learning by playing

Simulations and small games that let medical students fail safely, often, and with good humor — until the right answer starts to feel obvious.

Department IV

The math behind the kidney

A small equation makes big decisions about transplant lists and drug doses. We follow the slow, contested work of revising it for the people it has been quietly failing.
